# Lesson 6 complete: first pixel texture on the crate

Completed 2026-06-28 (completion requested by the learner). The learner reopened the Day-1 crate and
turned the flat-color cube into a readable pixel-textured prop: plank bands, dark wood seams, side
details, and cool metal corner caps.

## What is now established

- Texture detail can improve an asset without adding new geometry.
- The Paint Brush at a tiny size can add readable plank lines, edge shadows, and small marks.
- The shared palette from Lesson 5 is now being used as a constraint, not just a one-day exercise.
- A zoomed-out / thumbnail check matters: texture marks should clarify the prop, not make it noisy.

## Teaching implication

Do not repeat the same texture-detail pattern on another old prop. The learner wants each lesson to
introduce a new skill, and has already learned pivot/origin and basic export. Move next into beginner
UV mapping with a new, substantial kitchen prop: a chalkboard menu stand whose front face maps to a
specific `MENU` patch on the texture image.
