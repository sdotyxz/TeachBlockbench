# Lesson 4 complete: rotate for readable angles

Completed 2026-06-26 (evidence in repo; "finish plate spoon" commit). The learner built and rendered a
serving plate with a raised rim and a metal spoon lying diagonally across it (plus a small green garnish
cube), using typed `Element → Rotation` values (`Y -35`) to angle the spoon off the grid.

## What is now established

- Rotate is the third transform alongside Move and Resize; typed Rotation numbers give exact angles.
- The crucial distinction between **orbiting the camera** (view only) and **rotating an element** (the model).
- Matching the same rotation value across two cubes (handle + bowl) makes them read as one object.
- The kitchen set now has four props: crate, chopping board + knife, cooking pot, plate + spoon.
- Week 1 (Foundations & first props) is effectively complete.
