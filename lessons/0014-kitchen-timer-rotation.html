14Kitchen timer rotation - animate a swinging needle

You already know how to change Scale. Today the new skill is different: Rotation keyframes. You will build a tiny kitchen timer and animate only its needle, so you learn how pivots control animated turning.

Today's win

One 1-second looping GIF: a cute kitchen timer with a red needle swinging left, center, right, center, and left again.

Lesson target: the timer body stays still; the needle rotates around the center of the dial.

Warm up - 30-second recall

Answer first, then open the cards.

What is a keyframe?
A saved pose or value at a specific time in the animation.
Why did Lesson 13 animate a group?
A group keeps related parts moving together and makes the Timeline easier to debug.
What does a pivot control?
The pivot is the point an element or group rotates around.

The one new idea - rotation needs the right pivot

A Rotation keyframe stores an angle at a specific time. But rotation is only useful if the part turns around the right point. A timer needle should rotate around the center of the dial, not around one corner of the rectangle.

That gives today a simple rule: set the pivot first, then add Rotation keyframes. The official Blockbench Overview & Tips page describes Animate mode, the Timeline, the Keyframe Panel, and keyframe channels. The local Blockbench source also exposes transform channels for rotation, position, and scale.

Beginner boundary

Do not animate the whole timer. The timer body is the stage. The needle is the actor.

Do it with me

  1. New Generic Model - name it kitchen-timer-needle-loop

    Save early. This is a tiny animation study that can still become a pack prop later.

  2. Build a simple timer face

    Use a flat cube or low-poly cylinder as the timer body. Keep it front-facing. Add a darker rim and three small tick marks so viewers understand it is a dial.

  3. Build one red needle

    Add a thin red cuboid for the pointer. Place one end at the center of the timer face and point the other end upward. Add a small dark hub over the center.

  4. Group only the moving needle parts

    Put the red pointer and center hub in one Outliner group named timer_needle_group. Keep the timer body and tick marks outside the group.

  5. Set the needle pivot to the dial center

    Select timer_needle_group. Use the Pivot Tool or the pivot fields to put the pivot at the center of the dial. This is the point the needle will rotate around.

  6. Switch to Animate mode

    Add one animation named timer_needle_swing. Set the length to 1.0s.

  7. Keyframe 1: left at 0.00s

    Select timer_needle_group. Move the playhead to 0.00s. Add a Rotation keyframe with Z -35.

  8. Keyframe 2: center at 0.25s

    Move to 0.25s. Add a Rotation keyframe with Z 0.

  9. Keyframe 3: right at 0.50s

    Move to 0.50s. Add a Rotation keyframe with Z 35.

  10. Keyframe 4: center at 0.75s

    Move to 0.75s. Add a Rotation keyframe with Z 0.

  11. Keyframe 5: left at 1.00s

    Move to 1.00s. Add a Rotation keyframe with Z -35. The final angle matches the first angle, so the loop can restart cleanly.

  12. Preview and debug the pivot

    Turn on Looped Playback. If the needle orbits around the timer, stop and fix the pivot before changing keyframes. If the whole timer rotates, you keyed the wrong group.

  13. Capture a GIF

    Keep the camera fixed and record the timer needle swinging. A small, readable loop is enough.

  14. Post your Day-14 update

    Publish the GIF and a short note. Streak: Day 14.
Animation habit

For rotating parts, check the pivot before blaming the Timeline. Most beginner rotation bugs are pivot bugs.

Quick self-check

Say the answer first, then open the card.

What does a Rotation keyframe store?
It stores the selected group's angle at one time in the animation.
Why does the timer needle need a centered pivot?
The needle should rotate around the dial center, not around a corner or edge.
Why keep the final rotation equal to the first?
Matching start and end angles prevents a visible loop pop.
If the whole timer rotates, what is wrong?
The timer body was selected or grouped with the needle when the keyframe was added.
