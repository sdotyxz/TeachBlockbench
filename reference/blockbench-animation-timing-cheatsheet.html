Animation Timing Cheatsheet

A beginner-safe guide for making a tiny Blockbench loop feel less stiff with time placement, interpolation, and Scale keyframes.

Mental model

Position keyframes say where the prop is. Scale keyframes say how the prop changes shape. Timing is where those keyframes sit on the Timeline.

Beginner bounce recipe

Interpolation words

ModePlain meaningUse now?
linearEven change between keyframes.Yes. Predictable and easy to debug.
stepHold one value, then jump.No. Useful for snappy switches later.
catmullromSmoother curve through keyframes.No. Save it for later polish.
bezierCurve handles control easing.No. Powerful, but too much UI today.

Troubleshooting

SymptomLikely causeFix
The tomato grows forever.You scaled it in Edit mode.Undo, switch to Animate mode, then add Scale keyframes.
The bounce pops at the loop.The final keyframe does not match the first.Copy the 0.00s Position and Scale values to 1.00s.
The squash looks like a mistake.The scale values are too large.Use smaller changes: X/Z 1.08, Y 0.92.
The motion feels robotic.All keyframes are evenly spaced.Put recovery keyframes close to impact and keep a short hang at the top.
